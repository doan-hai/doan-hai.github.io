1. Which group is required to be at the Sprint Review? (choose the best answer)

  • The Product Owner, the Scrum Master and the Developers.
  • The Product Owner and stakeholders.
  • The Product Owner and Product Lead.
  • The Scrum Team and stakeholders. ✅
  • The Product Owner, technical leads and stakeholders.

The Sprint Review is an inspect and adapt opportunity for both the Scrum Team and key stakeholders.

2. True or False: The Product Owner is accountable for both maximizing the value of the product and the value of the work done by the Developers.

  • True ✅
  • False

The value of the work of the team is nothing but the value they create through the product. The Product Owner is responsible for maximizing the value the product delivers to the users and of the work of the Developers. How this is done may vary widely across organizations, Scrum Teams and individuals.

3. What is a Product Owner responsibility that he/she might delegate? (choose the best answer)

  • Writing User Stories
  • Ordering the Product Backlog ✅
  • Representing stakeholders to the Scrum team
  • Attending the Sprint Review

The Developers may need to help order the Product Backlog as they would know the most about technical limitations, risks, or dependencies between Product Backlog Items. Because User Stories is a localized practice, Scrum does not govern it or dictate the practice. How the team describes the items is up to them as long as it has enough information to be transparent and understood.

4. True or False: It is OK for the items at the bottom of the Product Backlog to have less detail compared to those that the top.

  • True ✅
  • False

Items on the bottom of the Product Backlog won't be developed soon, therefore, we don't need to spend too much time on them. Otherwise, it would be a form of upfront planning.

5. The Sprint Backlog is the set of Product Backlog items selected for the Sprint, plus a plan for delivering the product Increment and realizing the Sprint Goal. When can the Developers add, remove or change the work needed to complete the Increment and meet the Sprint Goal? (choose the best answer)

  • As soon as the Scrum Master identifies new work.
  • As soon as the Product Owner identifies new work.
  • Only at the Daily Scrum.
  • As soon as they are identified. ✅

The Sprint Backlog is a living artifact that evolves as more is learned.

6. Who supports the Scrum Master in removing impediments? (choose the best two answers)

  • The customer.
  • The senior management. ✅
  • The Director of Engineering.
  • Anyone on the Scrum Team. ✅

The Developers can help the Scrum Master because of their understanding of how the work is done and their internal interactions with each other. Some impediments are rooted in external causes and that's why the Scrum Master might need to have the management's support in removing them. The Product Owner can also support the Scrum Master by managing stakeholder expectations.

The database should be restored with a Recovery Time objective (RTO) of one minute when there is a service degradation in the primary region. The service restoration should incur the least admin work.

What approach can be initiated to design an Aurora database to meet cross-region disaster recovery requirements?

A. Use Amazon Aurora Global Database and use the secondary region as a failover for service degradation in the primary region

B. Use Multi-AZ deployments with Aurora Replicas which will go into failover to one of the Replicas for service degradation in the primary region
